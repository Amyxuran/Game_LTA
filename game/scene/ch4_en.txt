:A few nights later, just past midnight, Anna stands alone on the platform.;

:She has come here several times now, but the air tonight feels different. Denser. Colder. Charged in a way that tightens the skin, like the last few minutes before a storm breaks.;

:Night wind pours out from the tunnel carrying rust and apple scent together. By now she can tell them apart, the chill of metal and the sweetness of fruit woven into a single dissonant, irresistible chord.;

:The vending machine's glow pulses on and off, slower than before, like the heartbeat of something gathering itself. Whenever it brightens, a warm yellow veil spreads over the walls and floor of the platform. Whenever it dims, darkness pours back in from both ends of the tunnel and surrounds her again.;

:Anna stands exactly on that border between light and dark and feels everything converging into this one moment.;

:Jonathan's sketchbook. Emily's words. Philip's brief hesitation. The inexplicable photograph still in her phone. The stranger in the hallway carrying instant noodles. Her own reflection in the subway window.;

:All of those fragments whirl through her mind like a shattered kaleidoscope. Every angle makes a different pattern, and none of them are whole.;

:She knows she is standing at a crossroads. Not the kind with clear road signs, but the kind you find in an empty field at midnight where every direction is equally dark.;
