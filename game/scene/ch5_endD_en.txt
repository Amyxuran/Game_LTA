:The city is waking up. In the distance, a sanitation worker pushes a cart past. A few sparrows peck at crumbs beside the tracks. The skyline is draped in a thin blue-grey, like a veil just lifted.;

:Her phone buzzes. A text from Emily:;

:"You made the right choice.";

:Anna stares at the message for a long time. The right choice. She flips the phone face-down.;

:What does "right" even mean? Will Jonathan still sit on that bench and smile? The people who ate the apples — they'll be labeled victims, forced into "treatment," forced back into a world of anxiety and competition. Is that salvation?;

:A commuter walks past her, hurried steps, eyes sweeping over the corner where the vending machine once stood — now just a clean tile and four screw holes in the wall. He notices nothing. Head down, he keeps walking.;

:That's how the world works. Someone changes everything. Someone else doesn't notice a thing.;

:Anna stands up and pulls her jacket zipper to the top. The wind on the platform is cold. She doesn't know if what she did was right, but she knows one thing —;

:The moment truth is spoken aloud, it no longer belongs to you. It grows its own roots, blooms flowers you can't predict. Whether those flowers are beautiful or ugly is no longer yours to decide.;

:That is the cost of truth. And its meaning.;
