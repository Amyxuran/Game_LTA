Emily:...You're the first person who's ever asked me that.;

Emily:Two years ago, I was a postdoc in computer science at NYU. My research was neural networks and behavioral prediction. My advisor had a... very ambitious project.;

Emily:He convinced me to join a secret project, using AI models to design nanoscale delivery carriers that could encapsulate compounds modulating dopamine signaling inside natural fruit. We called it Project EDEN.;

Emily:The original idea was to treat severe anxiety disorders and addiction. But later the project was taken over by another institution. They wanted to study what would happen if high-performing elites collectively lost their desire to compete.;

Emily:I was the one who ate the first apple then. Not for the experiment. Because I was also about to collapse.;

Emily:For me, the effect lasted only three days. Maybe because I understood the mechanism too well. But those three days... were the calmest three days of my life.;

Emily:So I stayed here. Not to observe the experiment, but to see whether anyone could find that kind of peace without depending on the apple.;
