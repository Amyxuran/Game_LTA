:Day one. Library.;

:Anna takes her usual old seat by the fifth-floor window. In front of her lie three reference books, two printed papers, and a laptop with a crack in one corner of the screen. She makes herself a rule: today she will write at least two thousand words of literature review.;

:The coffee comes from the vending machine on the first floor, served in paper cups stamped with the library crest. The first cup is gone by ten in the morning, the second by two in the afternoon, the third by six in the evening. Each one tastes more bitter than the last and helps less than the last.;

:Outside the window, the sky changes from gray-white to dark blue to absolute black. She writes fourteen hundred words, deletes six hundred, and ends with a net gain of eight hundred. Half her goal is still missing, but she can no longer read the words on her screen, not because her eyes are tired, but because the letters have begun to slide across her retina like reflections on water.;

changeBg:bg-lab.jpg -next;

:Day two. Lab.;

:Anna sits in the corner of the lab, surrounded by used noodle bowls and empty coffee cups. She tries to run a new set of data analyses, but the SPSS output is nothing like what she expected. The effect size is too small, the p-value hovering at the threshold of significance like it can't decide which side to fall on.;

:She falls asleep with her head on the desk. When she wakes, the marks of the keyboard are imprinted across her cheek, and the screensaver has turned the monitor into a rotating field of stars. Staring at those artificial stars, for one dazed second she thinks they are glowing yellow.;

:The vending machine is in her head again.;

:She shakes herself hard and drags the cursor back to the data table.;

:Her phone vibrates. The lab group chat. She doesn't want to look, but her thumb has already opened it.;

:"Congrats, Liu!!! JPSP accepted the paper!!!";

:A flood of congratulatory messages follows, emojis, and a screenshot Liu posts of the editor's email: "We are pleased to inform you..." ;

:Anna flips the phone face-down. Something contracts in her chest, like a hand closing around it. Not jealousy, or not only jealousy. More like a reminder: look, everyone else is moving, and you are still standing still.;

changeBg:bg-lab-night.jpg -next;

:2:13 a.m. After staring at the outline of her paper for forty straight minutes, Anna suddenly has an idea.;

:She starts typing with a rush of excitement, fingers flying over the keyboard, her thoughts pouring out like a pipe suddenly cleared. One paragraph, then two, then three.;

:Then she stops.;

:She flips back to Philip's comments from last month. On page three, in red ink: "I discussed this direction at a conference in 2019. The conclusion was that it is not feasible. Rethink." ;

:The exact same idea. She has spent three days walking a giant circle just to return to the point her advisor rejected long ago.;

:Anna shuts the laptop. The snap of the screen closing sounds unnaturally loud in the empty office.;

:She closes her eyes. What rises in her mind is not the data table, not the structure of her paper, but the screen of the vending machine, the perfect red apples, Emily's voice, that yellow light.;

:She does not open the computer again.;
