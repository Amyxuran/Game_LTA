:Philip is still sitting at his desk, typing in a steady rhythm, unhurried and exact. Anna stands there, rehearsing her opening line three times in her mouth. What comes out is something completely different.;

setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Anna:Philip, if someone realizes the thing they're chasing isn't what they truly want, is giving it up a failure?;

:Philip's hands stop on the keyboard. He doesn't turn around right away. The light from the screen reflects in his lenses like two small windows.;

setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;

:Slowly, Philip turns his chair. The expression on his face is the one Anna knows well: calm, appraising, edged with impatience. But when he speaks, his voice is half a note lower than usual.;

Philip:...Are you talking about Jonathan? Or about yourself?;

setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Anna:Maybe both.;

:Even she didn't expect herself to be that direct. The moment the words leave her mouth, the air in the office seems to lock in place.;

setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Philip:Anna, you are at a critical stage of your doctoral program. Every graduate student goes through this kind of existential crisis. The ones who make it through are not the ones who start questioning their direction at this point.;

setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Anna:Jonathan went through this stage too. Then he left. Do you think he couldn't endure it, or do you think he saw something the rest of us didn't?;

setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;

:Philip's jaw tightens. Anna sees his hand grip the armrest of his chair, just slightly, but she sees it.;

setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Philip:Jonathan was a talented student. But talent is not the same thing as stamina. Academia requires more than intelligence. It also requires...;

:He stops. It is not the pause of someone arranging his words. It is the pause of something catching in his throat.;

:Anna has never seen Philip pause. He is the kind of man who always knows the next sentence before the current one ends. In defenses, conference talks, even casual conversation, his language always sounds prewritten, precise, smooth, airtight.;

:But right now, he stops.;

setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Philip:...The day he left, he sat in my office for an entire afternoon. He didn't say a single word. He just sat there.;

:The tone of that sentence is completely different from everything before it. It is no longer an advisor scolding a student. It is... what? Anna cannot tell. Regret? Confusion? Or something deeper she does not want to guess at?;

:But the crack lasts only a second. It is as if Philip reboots himself. His expression returns to its familiar severity.;

setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Philip:Anna, I'm not telling you this so you'll sympathize with him. Academia has rules. You can question them, but you cannot question them and expect them to bend around you at the same time.;

Philip:Your first full draft is due next Friday. If you think the direction is wrong, we can discuss it. But giving up is not on the table.;

setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;

:Anna looks at him. Under the fluorescent light, she notices details she had never really seen before. More gray at his temples than last semester. Deeper lines around his eyes when he isn't speaking. A wrinkle at the collar of his shirt, which on someone usually so immaculate is almost a scream.;

:He is struggling too. Anna realizes it all at once. She doesn't know with what. Jonathan's leaving, maybe. Maybe something else. Maybe the fact that academia is slowly grinding everyone down and no one wants to say it aloud.;

:But she doesn't say it. Some things, once spoken, only make two people hurt more.;

setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":0.4} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;
Anna:I understand.;

setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-left -duration=300 -next;
setTransform:{"brightness":1} -target=fig-right -duration=300 -next;

:The professor's gaze is still sharp. But the blade doesn't shine the way it used to.;

:Anna turns toward the door. The moment her hand touches the handle, a very soft sound comes from behind her, almost like a sigh, almost like the back of a chair settling under someone's weight.;

:She doesn't turn around.;

:The fluorescent light in the hallway hits her face the second she steps outside, white and painful. Her footsteps echo through the empty corridor, every step repeating the same question:;

:If giving up is not an option, where exactly is she walking toward?;

:She does not know the answer.;
