:At eleven o'clock that night, Anna gets on the subway again.;

:There are only a few passengers left in the car: a young man with headphones on and his eyes shut, two drunk men muttering to each other, and a woman holding a cello case. Everyone floats inside their own sealed little world. No one looks at her.;

:Anna grips her phone. The text message is still on the screen: "If you still want answers, tonight at 11:45. Same platform. --E" She has no idea how Emily got her number.;

:The train moves through the tunnel, the lights flickering overhead. Anna's heartbeat is faster than usual. Not exactly fear. More like the feeling of standing at the edge of a diving board, toes already hanging over the drop while the rest of the body hasn't caught up yet.;

:She asks herself: what exactly are you doing? Taking the subway in the middle of the night to meet a stranger you've spoken to only once?;

:But another voice answers: you're already here.;

:The platform looks completely different this late at night. Whatever traces of daytime human presence remained are gone. The air is cold and dry, carrying that mineral smell peculiar to underground stations. The fluorescent lights are dimmer than she remembers, and two of them have gone completely dead, leaving black rectangular wounds in the ceiling.;

:But the vending machine is brighter than ever.;

:That yellow isn't LED white, isn't fluorescent cold. It's a warm, almost tangible kind of light. It spills out from the screen, pooling across the floor like soft luminous water. The word "EDEN" breathes in the glow, brightening and fading with the rhythm of a human chest.;

:Anna stops at the mouth of the platform without stepping into that light. She looks farther down it instead--;

changeFigure:char-emily.png -right -next;

:Emily is sitting at the far end of the platform, leaning against a pillar with her legs folded, a book open across her knees. Her posture is loose and natural, as if she were sitting in her own living room instead of an abandoned midnight subway station.;

:Anna's footsteps echo in the open space. Emily looks up and closes the book.;

:From twenty meters away, Anna can see her smile. It isn't polite, and it isn't triumphant. It's the expression of someone who already knew she would come, quiet and certain.;
