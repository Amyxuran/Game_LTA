:After the press conference, Anna stands alone on the platform. It is five in the morning. Train service has not started yet.;

:The city is waking up. A sanitation worker pushes a cart in the distance. Sparrows peck along the tracks. A pale blue-gray veil lies over the skyline as if the day has just begun pulling itself into existence.;

:Her phone vibrates. A text from Emily:;

:"You made the right choice.";

:Anna stares at the message for a long time. The right choice. She turns the phone face-down.;

:What does "right" even mean? Will Jonathan still sit on that bench smiling? Will the people who ate the apples now be classified as victims, forced into treatment, forced back into a world of anxiety and competition? Is that rescue?;

:A commuter passes by her quickly, glancing at the corner where the vending machine once stood. There is nothing there now except clean tile and four screw holes in the wall. He notices nothing and keeps moving.;

:Anna rises and zips her coat to the top. She doesn't know whether what she did was right. But she knows one thing:;

:The moment truth is spoken aloud, it no longer belongs to the person who carried it. It grows roots of its own and blooms in ways no one can predict. Whether those flowers will be beautiful or monstrous is no longer hers to decide.;

:The wind on the platform is cold.;
